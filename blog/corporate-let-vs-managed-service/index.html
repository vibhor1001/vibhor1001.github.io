Corporate Let vs Managed Service: Which Model Suits Your Property?

By PropertyFlow Team·March 20, 2026·7 min read

Update: PropertyFlow now offers a single self-service platform plan.

This article describes our previous service tiers (Corporate Let and Managed Service). PropertyFlow has since simplified to a single platform offering. Learn about our current platform →

One of the first questions Edinburgh landlords asked when exploring short-term let management was simple: hand the property over entirely, or stay involved and take a larger share of the revenue? PropertyFlow offered both options — a Corporate Let agreement and a Managed Service model — because there was no one-size-fits-all answer. The right choice depended on a landlord's financial goals, risk tolerance, and how involved they wanted to be in their property's operation.

This archived guide explains how each structure worked, who bore which costs and risks, and the type of landlord each model was designed for.

The Corporate Let Model: Guaranteed Rent (Subject to Lease Terms), Zero Hassle

Under a Corporate Let agreement, PropertyFlow became the tenant of the property. PropertyFlow signed a lease directly with the owner, paid a guaranteed monthly rent (subject to lease terms), and took on full operational responsibility for the property as a short-term let.

This was not a management agreement — it was a tenancy. PropertyFlow was the tenant, and sublicensed the property to guests through its platform and a network of booking channels. The owner received a fixed monthly payment, subject to the lease terms, regardless of occupancy, seasonality, or market conditions.

What the Corporate Let Included

  • ✓Guaranteed monthly rent (subject to lease terms) — paid regardless of occupancy or bookings
  • ✓All bills covered by PropertyFlow — council tax, utilities, broadband, and TV licence were PropertyFlow's responsibility
  • ✓Vacancy risk absorbed by PropertyFlow — if the property sat empty, PropertyFlow still paid the rent due under the lease
  • ✓Full STL compliance — licensing, safety certificates, planning requirements, and regulatory filings handled by PropertyFlow
  • ✓Compliance costs borne by PropertyFlow — STL licence fees, fire safety, legionella checks, and insurance costs were covered by PropertyFlow
  • ✓Comprehensive AXA insurance — public liability, employer's liability and professional indemnity cover
  • ✓Multi-platform distribution — Airbnb, Booking.com, Google Vacation Rentals, Trip.com, Hopper Homes, and more

Who Was the Corporate Let For?

The Corporate Let model suited landlords who wanted complete predictability. For owners with a mortgage to service, or those who simply wanted passive income without any operational involvement, this was the model of choice. They knew what they would receive each month under the lease, and bore no risk from seasonal fluctuations, cancellations, or periods of low demand.

It was also the usual choice for owners living outside Edinburgh, overseas investors, or anyone who did not want the administrative burden of STL licensing and compliance. PropertyFlow handled everything — the owner simply collected the rent.

The Managed Service Model: Higher Potential, Shared Risk

Under the Managed Service model, the landlord remained the owner and operator of the property. PropertyFlow acted as management agent, handling all day-to-day operations — guest management, pricing, cleaning, maintenance, compliance, and channel distribution — for a management fee.

The key difference from the Corporate Let was that the owner received the booking revenue directly (minus the management fee and platform fee), which meant income varied with occupancy and seasonal demand. In strong months — Edinburgh Festival, Hogmanay, Six Nations weekends — owners earned significantly more than they would have under a guaranteed rent (subject to lease terms) arrangement. In quieter months, income was lower.

What the Managed Service Included

  • ✓Management fee — a transparent percentage of booking revenue
  • ✓Higher earning potential — owners kept the majority of revenue, benefiting from peak-season pricing
  • ✓Full operational management — guest communications, check-in/out, cleaning, linen, maintenance
  • ✓Full STL compliance — licensing, safety certificates, and regulatory filings managed by PropertyFlow
  • ✓Compliance costs invoiced transparently — owners saw every cost line item in their monthly statement
  • ✓Comprehensive AXA insurance — same cover: public liability, employer's liability and professional indemnity cover
  • ✓Full multi-platform distribution — same channel reach as the Corporate Let
  • ✓AI-powered dynamic pricing — rates optimised daily based on demand, events, and competitor data

Who Was the Managed Service For?

The Managed Service was designed for landlords who wanted to maximise their earning potential and were comfortable with variable income. For a mortgage-free property, or owners with other income sources who could tolerate quieter months, the Managed Service typically delivered higher annual returns than a Corporate Let — because it captured the upside of Edinburgh's peak demand periods.

It also suited owners who wanted transparency and visibility into their property's performance. They received detailed monthly statements showing every booking, every cost, and every revenue line, so they could see exactly how the property was performing and make data-driven decisions about the investment.

Which Model Delivered Better Returns?

The honest answer was: it depended on the property, its location, and market conditions. For a well-located Edinburgh city-centre property with strong year-round demand, the Managed Service typically delivered 15-25% more annual revenue than a Corporate Let — because it captured festival season premiums, Hogmanay rates, and event-driven spikes that a guaranteed rent figure could not account for.

However, the Corporate Let delivered something the Managed Service could not: predictability. An owner with a £1,200 monthly mortgage payment and a guaranteed rent (subject to lease terms) of £1,800 could plan around the mortgage being covered each month, whatever happened in the market. That peace of mind had real value, particularly for leveraged investors or owners who depended on rental income.

Could Landlords Switch Between Models?

Landlords could move between the Corporate Let and Managed Service models, subject to the notice periods set out in their agreement. Some started with a Corporate Let for the first year to establish a baseline income while they assessed the market, then switched to the Managed Service once they were confident in the property's performance. Others did the reverse — starting with the Managed Service and moving to a Corporate Let when they wanted to simplify their financial planning.

PropertyFlow was transparent about the trade-offs so landlords could choose the model that genuinely suited their circumstances. There was no pressure to pick one over the other — both were fully supported by the platform, the compliance infrastructure, and the operational team.

How Landlords Made the Decision

The choice usually came down to three questions:

  1. Did the landlord need predictable income? If so, the Corporate Let removed the month-to-month variability. PropertyFlow paid the rent each month (subject to lease terms), handled the running costs, and absorbed the occupancy risk.
  2. Were they comfortable with variable income for higher returns? If so, the Managed Service let them capture peak-season revenue while PropertyFlow handled all operations.
  3. How involved did they want to be? Both models were hands-off, but the Managed Service gave more visibility and control over pricing decisions, while the Corporate Let was truly passive.

Both models have since been retired. PropertyFlow now offers a single self-service platform, with a genuine 50/50 Partner programme (by application) for owners who want a hands-off arrangement.
