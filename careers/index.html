About PropertyFlow

PropertyFlow Technologies Ltd was founded in December 2025 in Edinburgh. We're a PropTech startup building a cloud-based platform for short-term rental operators -- automating multi-channel distribution across major booking platforms like Airbnb and Booking.com, AI-Powered Cognito Pricing, and compliance tracking for UK STL regulations.

Alongside the platform, we offer property management as a service to landlords -- either as a corporate let or fully managed service. We're scaling rapidly, targeting Edinburgh's growing addressable market.

The team is small, the pipeline is growing fast, and we're looking for people who want to build something from the ground up.

Why Work Here

Early-Stage Impact

We're pre-launch and scaling fast. You'll build functions from scratch, not inherit someone else's playbook. Every team member shapes the company directly.

Compliance-First Culture

We do things properly. Edinburgh's STL licensing regime is complex, and we've built our entire approach around regulatory compliance. Rigour matters here.

Technology-Driven

Cognito pricing, automated compliance tracking, multi-channel distribution. We're a technology company that happens to work in property, not the other way around.

Genuine Flexibility

Edinburgh-based with real hybrid flexibility. The office is our base, but we trust our team to work where they're most productive. No rigid desk hours.

Autonomy & Ownership

We hire people we trust and let them run. Side projects and independent thinking are encouraged. You'll have real commercial exposure and direct impact on growth.

Visa Sponsorship

Skilled Worker visa sponsorship is available for eligible senior roles. We're open to international talent who bring the right experience and drive.

Property Portfolio Manager (Senior)

Edinburgh (On-site) Full-time, Permanent Competitive salary Reports to CEO

This is a strategic leadership role driving PropertyFlow's expansion into direct property acquisition alongside SaaS platform growth. You'll own a portfolio of commercial projects -- acquisitions, renovation programmes, compliance cycles, and platform onboarding -- with full accountability for delivery.

Start date: September 2026. 37.5 hours per week, Monday to Friday. Skilled Worker visa sponsorship available for the right candidate.

Key Responsibilities

  • • Own the property acquisition pipeline end to end -- source, evaluate, and project-manage the purchase and conversion of residential units into licensed short-term lets.
  • • Conduct site assessments using civil engineering / building surveying expertise, evaluating structural condition, renovation requirements, and cost estimation.
  • • Manage the compliance calendar across all properties -- STL licence renewals, gas safety certificates (CP12), EICR reports, EPC ratings, PAT testing, fire safety, and legionella risk assessments.
  • • Drive SaaS platform growth by onboarding new property owners from Edinburgh's growing addressable market.
  • • Coordinate cross-functional teams across operations, commercial, technology, and on-the-ground property management. Prepare portfolio-wide performance reports and strategic recommendations for the CEO and board.

Essential Requirements

  • ✓ Degree-level qualification (RQF Level 6+) in civil engineering, construction management, building surveying, or business management
  • ✓ 10+ years project management experience managing multi-stakeholder programmes with budgets, timelines, and deliverables
  • ✓ Technical knowledge of building assessment: structural integrity, renovation scoping, cost estimation, and building safety regulations
  • ✓ Experience with regulatory compliance in property or construction (safety certificates, licensing, planning permissions)
  • ✓ Financial management skills: P&L oversight, acquisition business cases, portfolio performance reporting

Desirable

  • + MBA or equivalent postgraduate business qualification
  • + Experience with short-term rental, hospitality, or property technology platforms
  • + Knowledge of Scottish STL licensing regulations and Edinburgh's Control Area requirements
  • + Experience managing large-scale government or institutionally funded projects
