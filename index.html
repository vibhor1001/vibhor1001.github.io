Frequently Asked Questions

If you let a property in Scotland on a short-term basis (under 31 consecutive days), you need a Short-Term Let licence under the Civic Government (Scotland) Act 1982. In Edinburgh, you also need planning permission for secondary letting. PropertyFlow manages the entire process.
Our standard commission is 5%. For the first 1,000 properties onboarded, we're offering a founding rate of 3% — locked in for the lifetime of your agreement. Once spots fill, new landlords pay the standard rate.
With a Corporate Let, PropertyFlow becomes your tenant — we sign the lease, pay guaranteed rent monthly, and handle everything. With our Managed Service, you remain the landlord and we manage bookings, compliance, and property care for a competitive management fee.
Planning refusal is expected for most Edinburgh tenement flats (97.2% refusal rate — STL Solutions, 2025). Our proven compliance strategy ensures continuous legal operation regardless of planning outcomes. We handle all applications and appeals automatically.
You need specialist buildings insurance that permits STL use — standard home or buy-to-let policies don't cover it. PropertyFlow carries comprehensive AXA business insurance: £10,000,000 Public Liability, £5,000,000 Employer's Liability, and £100,000 Professional Indemnity per claim. You still need your own buildings and contents cover for your property — you still need your own buildings and contents cover. We recommend Pikl, Homeprotect, or Schofields for landlord-specific STL insurance.
Every guest is screened before they book. We use ID verification, review history checks, and a guest rating system. During stays, we monitor occupancy levels and noise in real-time.
From 24 July 2026, a 5% levy applies to the first 5 consecutive nights of any stay (this cap is removed from 25 January 2027, after which the levy applies to all nights). The levy applies to stays booked on or after 1 October 2025. Providers retain 2% of levy collected for administration. PropertyFlow handles collection, reporting, and quarterly remittance on your behalf.
