One market, four sets of rules.

A quick picture of where UK short-term let regulation stood as this page was written. A general summary only — always confirm the current rules with your local authority.

SCOTLAND

Mandatory licensing, live now

Every host needs a short-term let licence from their local authority. The scheme has applied since October 2022, and letting without one is a criminal offence carrying fines of up to £2,500.

ENGLAND

A national register, incoming

A national registration scheme is targeted for 2026, with a registration number shown on listings and a new C5 planning use class proposed alongside it.

LONDON

The 90-night cap

Whole-property short-term letting is limited to 90 nights per calendar year without planning permission.

WALES

Registration from 2026

A visitor-accommodation register opens in 2026, the first stage of reform that also brings a visitor levy and, later, statutory licensing.

A general summary, not legal advice. PropertyFlow keeps your documents and renewal dates in one place; Partners can hand the paperwork to our team.
