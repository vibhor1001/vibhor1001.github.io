Zero-Gap Compliance Strategy

Edinburgh's STL licensing system creates a compliance trap: Your temporary licence expires before your full licence is determined. This creates a gap where your property is unlicensed and you can face fines.

PropertyFlow's solution: Simultaneous submission

We submit your temporary licence, full licence application, and planning application all at the same time. The Council interprets this as continuous operation and extends your temporary licence protection until the full application is determined — no gap.

"If you have also made an application for a full licence at the same time, your temporary licence can be for longer than six weeks and last until your full licence application is determined."

Scottish Government, STL Licensing Scheme Part 1 Guidance, Paragraph 1.22

When Planning is Refused: Three Scenarios

Planning refusal is common in Edinburgh (97.2% refusal rate for tenement flats). PropertyFlow is prepared for all three outcomes:

Scenario 1: Planning Refused While Holding Full Licence

This is the most likely outcome. Your full STL licence is granted, but planning permission is refused.

What happens: We lodge an appeal to the Local Review Body (LRB). The Council keeps your file open while the appeal is determined. During this "planning cycling" period, your STL licence remains valid and you can operate.

Timeline: LRB determination typically takes 8–12 weeks.

Scenario 2: Full STL Licence Refused

Your STL licence application is refused by the Council (e.g., on grounds of overprovision or nuisance).

What happens: We file an appeal to the Sheriff Court within 28 days. Sheriff appeals are robust and overturn around 30–40% of refusals on procedural or factual grounds.

Timeline: Sheriff determination typically takes 4–6 months.

Scenario 3: Planning Enforcement Notice

A neighbour reports you for unlicensed operation, and the Council issues an enforcement notice telling you to stop.

What happens: We appeal the notice to the Development Plan Examination Authority (DPEA). Enforcement appeals are discretionary and require detailed evidence, but many succeed on grounds of planning policy shifts or material changes in circumstances.

Timeline: DPEA determination typically takes 6–9 months. You can continue operating while the appeal is live.
